Heavier than Air: Experiences of LGBTQ teachers represented through performance

With the explosion of interest in the experiences of young people at school, it was only a matter of time until we turned our eyes to the experiences of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer-identified (LGBTQ) teachers. ‘Heavier than Air’ involves animating transcripts from real-life teachers and asks audience members to critically consider the ‘performance’ of being an LGBTQ teacher in today’s schools.

A new, easier and more effective pathway to wellbeing

The Centre for Positive Psychology hosts its second public lecture for 2016 presented by Dr Aaron Jarden, Senior Lecturer, Psychology, Auckland University of Technology (AUT) and President of the New Zealand Association of Positive Psychology.

At this presentation Dr Aaron Jarden proposes and outlines a novel and largely unexplored pathway to wellbeing – simplifying your life.  The notion of simplification challenges the very essence of the existing paradigm which is to add activities to one’s life (to be more, to do more) and has the potential to be one of the most effective and efficient pathways to enhancing wellbeing.

Workforce literacy and factories of the future

Dean's Lecture Series 2016

Presented by Professor Lesley Farrell

The rise of robotics in manufacturing requires workers to have sophisticated literacy skills so they can direct machines and join up innovation and production networks. With machines doing the work that people used to do, the way products are imagined, developed, produced and distributed is being transformed, creating new work for people.

Shifting away from distractions to improve Australia's schools

Dean's Lecture Series 2016

Jack Keating Memorial Lecture
Presented by Laureate Professor John Hattie

The too long school reforms have been focused on structural solutions such as curricula, assessment, class size, autonomy and parental choice. However, after decades of investing in these popular policy fixes the best that can be said for Australia is that the achievement and engagement of our students is declining. This lecture argues for the need to invest in teacher education, and the expertise of teachers and school leaders.
