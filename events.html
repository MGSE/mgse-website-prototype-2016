Reading adventures

The University Library holds a rich selection of adventure books, from early examples such as Gulliver's Travels and Robinson Crusoe, to stories set across Britain's far-flung empire. Mapping the rise of the genre, the exhibition Reading Adventures will showcase a broad range of adventure narratives from the University Library's McLaren, Morgan and Public School Fiction collections. The exhibition will examine the growing popularity of adventure fiction during the nineteenth century, as printed books became more readily available and literacy rates rose. Featuring a range of lavish covers and attractive engravings, Reading Adventures will address how adventure stories developed in response to a changing body of readers. The exhibition will also include a public lecture program featuring the curator's talk and an exploration of how a new genre of fiction emerged in mid to late nineteenth century England.

Issues and ideas: Academic work from a comparative perspective

Academics are central to the knowledge society as producers of knowledge and trainers of knowledge workers, but the academic profession faces considerable challenges. Many academics lament the perceived increased managerial influence as undermining the traditional collegial norms which previously united academics across disciplines, institutions and countries. If professors continue to enjoy universal status, analogous to rabbis or priests, what is it that unites the professoriate?

This seminar in the Issues and Ideas in Higher Education series will discuss research findings, as well as the challenges of conducting research on the academic profession.

International conference on language policy in multicultural and multilingual settings

As part of the UNICEF/University of Melbourne Language, Education and Social Cohesion project, an international conference is to be held at the University of Mandalay, Myanmar.

Distinguished international and local speakers will present papers on aspects of multilingual language policy and its implications in the Asia Pacific region and more widely. Official representatives from across Southeast Asia will report on the state of multilingual education and language policy in diverse countries, while panels of academics, civil society organisation representatives and education practitioners will address critical issues and development in language policy and planning.  The organisers invite scholars and practitioners to submit proposals on multilingual education, language policy, social cohesion, and peacebuilding themes.

Australian Association for Research in Education (AARE) history and education research workshop

This special interest group workshop explores current research approaches in the history of education from theoretical and methodological debates to practical strategies and reflections from historians in action. It includes sessions on archives and digital records and also panel presentations. The keynote address is from Professor Thomas Popkewitz, University of Wisconsin-Madison, a leading cultural historian of education and curriculum. His work includes comparative, historical and ethnographic studies of national educational reforms and educational sciences.
